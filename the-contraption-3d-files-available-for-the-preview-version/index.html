The Contraption - 3D Files Available for the Preview Version

Mike and Becky November 24, 2025

The Contraption is a 3D printed case for creating a handheld HF radio out of a QRP radio + battery + antenna. It is ridiculous, but fun.

With the Contraption, you can turn your QMX or QCX-mini (and someday CFT1) into a handheld portable HF 20m radio. For morse code, you can use a magnetic key mounted to either side or plug an N6ARA TinyPaddle into the bottom. Soon, we will also release a magnetic mount on the side for a tripod mount phone holder.

The files are available now (of this early version) for anyone who wants to try it on their own 3D printer. We will continue to provide the files for free for those who wish to print their own, and we will soon be selling it as a kit with the 3D printed parts and hardware so that you don't have to source your own. Regardless, you will need to supply your own QRP radio, battery, antenna, morse code key, and cables.

Don't have a Just OK Mini antenna yet? go grab one as kit! You'll need one with The Contraption.

Disclaimer: Please enjoy this project at your own risk. Mike broke his favorite paddle already. Due to the nature of it, it is possible if not even likely that you drop something and break it. Here is an image showing the orientation we printed ours in. We printed on standard mode with 3 wall thickness and auto supports turned on. The filament is bamboo brand PETG - HF.

Other things you will need

  • QMX or QCX-mini (version for the CFT1 coming soon)
  • USB PD battery
  • Just OK Mini: ridiculously small 20m portable antenna
  • Morse code key with magnetic base or Ara's N6ARA tiny paddle
  • 2x of 2.5" x 1.42" x 0.07" steel plate
  • 1/4"-20 Heat Set Threaded Insert for the top antenna mount
  • tripod thread adapter to attach the top to the antenna
  • short 6" BNC cable to connect to the antenna to the radio
  • short right angle audio cable to connect the key to the radio (if using magnetic one on the side)
